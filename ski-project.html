Assembly Process

After 3D printing and laser cutting the tibia frame, I started assembling the prosthetic. I chose to use 5/16 bolts, with washers and nuts, to provide a strong clamping force—adding strength and security around the ankle region of the ski prosthetic.

Design details

Focus Areas

  • Anthropometric Fit: Scaling geometry and stance height to human leg proportions.
  • Binding Interface: Ensuring compatibility with standard ski bindings for safe release.
  • Mechanical Validation: FEA verification and bench testing to confirm stress and strain tolerances.
  • Structural Assembly: 5/16 fasteners, washers, and nuts selected for reliable ankle-region clamping force.

Methods

CAD modeling, structural FEA, and mechanical test correlation to validate design assumptions.

Outcome

A human-centered prosthetic ski design that fits naturally, integrates seamlessly, and withstands real-world loads.

Reference & Acknowledgment

Referenced Patent Inspiration

This concept was informed by prior art from U.S. Patent US7785373B2 — “Prosthetic device having flexing ankle joint.” The design was adapted and improved for ski-specific dynamics, emphasizing anthropometric fit, ski-binding integration, and mechanical integrity under load.
