Vancouver to San Francisco — On Two Wheels, For One Cause

July 2026

My American host family misses me. They keep asking when I'll visit the Bay Area. Once I land my first full-time role, I'll have my answer. I'm biking all the way down, partnering with the Canadian Cancer Society and the American Cancer Society to raise funds along the way.

A few days ago, I biked approximately 150 km (93.2 miles) past Clark Kent's house — the real filming location of Smallville, right here in the Lower Mainland. I grew up watching that show with my dad. My neighbour's husband worked on it for three years. And somehow I never realized Superman's hometown was in my backyard this whole time.

The ride I'm planning goes a little further.

Vancouver to San Francisco. The Lower Mainland to the Bay Area. Home to University of California, Berkeley. Through Seattle, through Portland, down the Pacific Coast — ocean cliffs, redwood forests, mountain passes. Over a week in the saddle. No flights. No shortcuts. Just two wheels and the same Pacific air I've been breathing my whole life.

And at the end of it: my host family's front door.

One ride. One cause. Both sides of the border.

I've been training for this longer than I knew. Back in my undergrad at The University of British Columbia, I took 7 courses a semester — back-to-back classes in different buildings, with only a bike to get me between them. Heart pounding, lecture to lecture. Biking made me stronger. It still does. That's the rigour of a Canadian engineering education — it's why we earn our Engineer-in-Training designation straight out of school.

The job search has been its own kind of endurance ride. Six interview processes with Apple, across six different positions. Now my first with Google, and referrals coming in at Rivian. In between, I've been studying, reviewing my undergrad fundamentals, staying healthy, and keeping my mind sharp the way I always have — on trails, on the bike, and with the people who matter.

The process taught me patience. Patience taught me a lot about myself.

I've driven every mile of this coast before. This time, I want to feel every one. On my own terms. The hard way. For something bigger than myself.

Some roads you drive. Some you have to earn.
